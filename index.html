How It Works
From a GitHub comment
to live on chain

No wallet setup on your end. No scripts. Just a comment.

1

Open an Issue

Go to the GitPump repo or any repo where the bot is installed and open an issue. Anyone with a GitHub account can participate.

2

Drop a Comment

Post a /launch command with your token name, symbol, description, target chain, and a logo image attached.

3

Bot Does the Rest

The GitPump bot downloads your logo, uploads it to IPFS, generates a fresh wallet, and deploys your token directly on your chosen chain — Pump.fun, Bags.fm, Clanker, or Four.meme.

4

Go Live

Your token is live on chain and appears on the gitpump.com live feed for everyone to see. Claim your creator fees any time with /claim.

For Your Repo
Install GitPump Bot
in Your Repo

Give your repo its own /launch command across all supported chains.

01

Install from GitHub Marketplace

Visit GitPump Bot and click Install. No credit card required. Free plan available.

02

Choose Your Repository

Select which repos get access — all repositories or specific ones. Change this any time from your GitHub App settings.

03

Open an Issue or PR

Create a new issue or use an existing one. The bot listens to all issue and PR comments in repos where it is installed.

04

Post the /launch Command

Write a comment with the command, pick your chain, and attach a logo image. GitHub account must be at least 14 days old.

05

Bot Replies with Your Token Link

GitPump uploads your logo to IPFS, deploys the token on your chosen chain, and replies with a direct link. Your token also appears on the gitpump.com live feed.
